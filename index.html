Step 2: Prototyping

  • Simple experimental model of a proposed solution
  • User stories
  • Low fidelity (Lo-fi) ‚Üí high fidelity (Hi-fi)

Armed with useful information about the people whose problem we were trying to solve, we started with digital product development process step 2: **Prototyping.**

The main goal of this step is to develop a prototype of your proposed solution, test it, gather feedback from your users and iterate, improving your prototype until you are satisfied with the solution.

It is very helpful to imagine how people will be using your product and who this people will be. For that purpose you can create imaginary personas and describe their journey (user flow) using your solution.

Prototypes usually go from very low fidelity (lo-fi) - sketches, storyboards, scribbles - to high fidelity (hi-fi) - detailed wireframes of proposed solution.

It is also important that you try to define a minimum viable product in this stage. This is particularly hard, because in this stage you want to include every possbile functionality in your product. But you have to be aware, that it is better to build a reliable product with fewer functionalities than unreliable one with plenty of functionalities. You really have to strip your product to its essentials and deliver most value with minimum effort.

Step 3: Visual design

All about implementing colors, typography, images and hierarchy in a way that enhances a design or interaction.

So we had our prototype and we tested in on different users to see if user journey flow is clear and that it makes sense to our users. Now it is time to tackle the third step of product development process: **Visual design.**

Visual design is all about implementing colors, typography, images, spacings in a way that enhances a design or interaction.

Two aspects of visual design in digital products

  • Build a brand image (control how people perceive your brand)
  • Make your interface a joy to use (people should experience positive emotions while using your product)

Visual design is all about implementing colors, typography, images, spacings in a way that enhances a design or interaction.

Two aspects of visual design in digital products:

  • Build a brand image (control how people perceive your brand)
  • Make your interface a joy to use (people should experience positive emotions while using your product)

If we look at Aarron Walters hierarchy of needs which is quite similar to Maslow‚Äôs hierarchy in psychology, we can see, that product must first be functional and reliable, only then can we worry about making it usable and pleasurable. Visual design is all about making product more usable and pleasurable.

To make product usable the designer should bring user‚Äôs attention to the right infromation and functionality, using different sizing, colors and typography. Important elements in the interface should be highlighted so user can seamlessly move through the journey.

Once the design is ready and whole team is satisfied the design has to be translated into code. That is the 4th step in digital product development: Technical development.

The main goal of this step is to turn design into a fully working and interactive solution. In terms of online digital products (web sites, online platforms, web apps) this usually involves:

  • - Architecting digital infrastructure (how all the services will communicate)
  • - Planning and building databases (how the data will be stored, structured and connected)
  • - Coding application‚Äôs backend (server side code which usually process, store and serve data to the client)
  • - Coding application‚Äôs frontend (client side code - coding interactive user interface)

Step 5: Measure and optimize

  1. Define your KPIs
  2. Set up tools to measure them
  3. Analyse & optimise
You have to think about your product KPIs. What metrics should we measure? Of course there are a lot of metrics that can tell you if your product is performing well - some of these metrics are: Number of users, time spent on page, bounce rate, traffic acqusition channels and others. But most importantly each product has to have a north star metric. This is metric that is the most important one and is directly linked to business success.

We are still figuring out what the north star metric will be for Kam za vikend product. Because we are planning to implement advertising business model our north star metric will probably be either traffic (number of users on our site) or time each user spends on page.

SEO optimisation

What your potential users will google for?

One other thing you need to figure out is SEO optimization. If you plan to acquire users through organic search (Google) you need to have some sort of SEO optimization in mind. You have to think about what your potential users will google for and then optimise your content accordingly.

For Kam za vikend, Google brings in the most traffic (34%), followed by Facebook and Instagram. We are also measuring which keywords people are seraching for and on which position we rank among google search results. We can thus see, for which keywords must we optimise.

Looking ahead

Building MVP is one thing. Growing and improving your product & business is another one.

Our objectives

  • Sustainable business model
  • Give users the ability to become content creators
  • Expanding content categories
  • Mobile application
  • Web 3.0

In 2022 we want to start building a sustainable business model for Kam za vikend and add features that will bring users even more value.

We have also learned that we can not gather all the infromation about the trips around Slovenia by ourselves that is why we will be giving more control to our users. We will give them an opportunity to create content with us and help us build the biggest trip ideas database in Slovenia.

We also plan to expand content categories, adding a lot of ideas that are currently not supported. Ideas for commercial tourist activities for example rafting, bungee jumping, ziplines etc. We intentionally left these ideas out, because we have to think about how will we monetize these ideas.

We also plan to create an mobile application which will open many doors for us, since we will be able to use mobile cameras and location services to prompt users about relevant trip ideas around them.

We are also looking into the recent hype about Web 3.0. We are looking for different possibilities on how to give users an opportunity to earn income by creating content on our platform. This certainly has become easier with payment platforms like Stripe or with blockchain and cryptocurrency technologies.

In the long run, anything is possible.

We will see where the path will take us.
